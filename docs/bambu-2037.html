The Thesis

Mexico buys the base. Export is upside.

Mexican demand ~217K poles/yr, split up and badly supplied — that's our spot. US export stops being a must-have and becomes the next step.

The Problem
The market exists but it's split up. Several producers in Mexico have real hectares, but none put together farm + treatment + logistics + spec sheet + sales to premium clients in one company. Buyers in Quintana Roo, Querétaro and CDMX end up buying from Colombia or moving poles between states with no guarantee of steady supply.
The Opportunity
Total MX demand ~217K poles/yr. Addressable to Pacífico ~95-100K. Realistic capture (stretch scenario, see XLSX Tab 9): ~64K poles/yr, ~36M MXN/yr — no US export dependency. Quintana Roo is the prize: ~85K poles/yr concentrated, all arriving from outside the state.
The Execution
3 phases. 2026-2028 builds trust through coastal presence, active distribution, and bids on government bamboo-construction projects. 2029 signs offtake contracts at locked price + Consumer Price Index clause before the projected bamboo price rise. 2031-2036 harvest comes in. 2037+ US channel opens with 7 years of relationship already built.
Underlying research: Mexico_Bamboo_Demand_Ecosystem.xlsx (11 tabs, 73 sources, validated 2026-05-03). Covers 32 states, panel + textile + 13 adjacent-niche ecosystems, bamboo vs tropical-hardwood pricing, 50K-pole capture model.

Phase 2 · 2029-2036

From trust to contracts: 50,000 poles/yr

Next · 2029 key year

The key year: sign 4-7 offtake contracts at locked price + Consumer Price Index before the expected bamboo price rise. Big harvest arrives in 2031.

The number scales from 5,500 to 50,000 poles/yr by fulfilling contracts, not hunting new clients.

① Offtake structure
4-7 contracts · locked price + Consumer Price Index
Categories: 1-2 boutique hospitality developer (10-15K poles/yr), 1 industrial processor (8-12K raw), 1-2 specialist architects (3-5K each), 1 national distributor (3-5K), 1 design-build cluster (2-3K). Avg price ~480 MXN. Clauses: price moves with Mexican Consumer Price Index + boron indexed to imported chemistry · annual volume floor · quarterly delivery schedule · quality spec (length, diameter, taper, moisture %) · force majeure · 90-day termination with cause.
② Production milestones
2031 cash-positive · 2033 break-even
2029-2030: 5,500-7,000 poles/yr (own harvest + distribution mix). 2031: 14,000 own poles — cash-positive. 2033-2034: ~35K poles/yr, cumulative break-even, dividends begin. 2035-2036: 50,000 poles/yr, 110 ha at full maturity. Per-key-account success metrics: % on-time delivery, biannual NPS, repeat order >70%.
③ Industrial capabilities
Slats 2030 · FSC 2031 · Showroom 2032
2030: slat processing line (Round 2 capex) — unlocks the industrial-processor offtake. 2031: FSC chain-of-custody complete. 2032: commercial showroom + offices. 2034: big roadside bodega for scale. Live data dashboard scales to multi-client: each key account gets its own login to see its pipeline.

Phase 3 · 2037+

US channel with 7 years of relationship already built

Long term · 2030 we start building relationships

Key milestone: ICC-ES AC162 closes late 2036. The first 2037 container lands with a buyer who's been visiting and receiving samples since 2030.

The product ladder climbs by certification level: decorative (2037) → engineered feedstock (2038-39) → structural premium (2040+).

① Decorative (2037)
No structural cert required
Buyer categories: US resort/hospitality importers and decorative distributors. Buy Guadua for tiki, palapa, decorative. Spec: 4-7m × 8-14cm Ø poles, boron-treated, non-structural. USMCA edge = 0% tariff vs Colombia. Year 1: ~4 containers (40 TEU equiv), ~2,400 poles, ~$80-120K USD.
② Engineered feedstock (2038-2039)
LVB processors · structural panels
Categories: LVB (laminated-veneer-bamboo) processors and hybrid panel makers. Buy billets + slats. Spec: 1m × 2cm × 0.6cm slats or 2m × 6×6cm billets. Pacífico = nearshore Mexican-grown vs the South American Guadua they import today. Year 2-3: 6-9 containers, ~$140-340K USD.
③ Structural premium (2040+)
Post-ICC-ES · eco-luxury architects
Categories: eco-luxury architects in California/Florida/Hawaii + high-end boutique hotel chains. Spec: structural poles 6m × 10-14cm Ø class A1, ICC-ES + ESR. Unlocks structural use under IBC code. Pacífico enters as second supplier with USMCA edge. 12+ containers/yr, ~$280-450K+ USD.
US logistics setup
Salina Cruz port 150 km from farm = real edge. MX + US customs broker assigned. Cargo insurance. Pack 1 setup (SEMARNAT + Padrón + APHIS) ~140K MXN one-time in 2036. Per container ~25K MXN (phyto + heat treatment + broker + freight share).
FX-hedged pricing
Contracts denominated in USD but cost is MXN. Quarterly FX adjustment clause if MXN/USD moves >5% from baseline. 90-180 day FX forwards for large orders. Target margin: 35-45% in USD after logistics.
Relationship-build 2030-2036
1 US trip/yr (Ofir) during 2030-2034. Non-sales visit. Key conferences (AIA, Greenbuild). Samples shipped to first prospect subset in 2031. ICC-ES pre-application meetings 2034-2036.

GTM Stress Test

What if the plan doesn't go as expected?

4 adverse scenarios over base (1,400 culms/ha, 400 MXN/pole). Under each: still profitable? does the GTM plan break?

Scenario Trigger Yield ASP Revenue / 110 ha Profitable? GTM mitigation
1 · Biological Shock Documented Guadua fungal disease (Bolaño-Garzón et al.) or extreme drought · -50% yield for 2 cycles 700 400 ~26M (-51%) YES 5 yrs pilot · 1,500 plants surviving of 3,000 · genetic diversity + 3 species
2 · Demand Softening 2029 offtakes fail or key accounts don't sign · ASP 400→250 MXN 1,400 250 (-37%) ~32.7M (-37%) YES Diversified channels redirect into stakes / biomass / decorative · secondary personas buy the volume
3 · Cashflow Delay Round 2 delays 12 mo; weather blocks 15 ha planting 2027 · maturity 2038→2040 no change no change -80M MXN cum. YES PRONAFOR PFC 30+20+50% covers up to 50% of planting capex · 40-yr lease ensures continuity
4 · Combined Adverse 3 simultaneous: yield -30%, ASP -25%, 1-yr delay 980 (-30%) 300 (-25%) ~25M (-52%) YES ~$1.18M USD/yr net · payback 7→9 yrs · thesis intact
Conclusion: even under 3 simultaneous stresses, the model is profitable. The thesis breaks only if simultaneously: (a) all 3 pilot species die, (b) all 5 channels close, (c) Round 2 fails. Joint probability < 1%. Resilience by design: 2029 key offtakes + diversification + CONAFOR + 5 yrs validated R&D.

Sources: Céspedes Prieto et al. 2020 (SciELO Colombia) · Camargo NPV 2021 · CONAFOR PFC · 5-yr pilot data — Pacífico Bambu El Azulillo (April 2021–April 2026, internal)
