The Problem

No Reliable Supply Chain Exists

US bamboo manufacturers depend on fragmented, uncertified, unreliable supply, mostly from Asia.

01
Fragmented Supply
~7,000 ha of bamboo in Mexico (CONAFOR). Larger plantations exist: Tosepan 450 ha (indigenous coop) and Volkswagen 350 ha (environmental compliance). But none export structural Guadua to the US. Commercial growers operate at 15-90 ha, all domestic. No supplier can guarantee export-grade volume, consistency, or quality.
02
No Vertical Integration
Nobody controls growing + treatment + distribution. Growers sell raw, untreated poles. Builders source randomly.
03
Zero Export Certification
No Mexican producer has ICC-ES or ASTM certification. The US market door is closed without it.
04
Asia Dependence = Risk
US builders source from China/Vietnam. 30-45 day shipping, tariff uncertainty, ESG concerns. They want nearshore alternatives.

Data-Driven Agronomy

Every Plant. Tracked. Visible.

Sensors monitor our plantation around the clock. The data is always live: for our team, for customers placing orders, and for investors.

01 · Every plant, every stage

From new shoot to mature culm, every plot is followed across its full life cycle: emergence, growth, harvest, regrowth.

02 · Always-on, open data

Agronomic dashboard available anytime. Customers placing orders see exactly what they're buying. Investors see what they own, in real time.

03 · Transparent yield data

A US architect can't fly to Oaxaca every month. Live data, photos, and harvest records replace the trip, and beat what any Mexican competitor offers today.

04 · Data-driven cultivation

Every season of data sharpens our agronomy: when to irrigate, where yields rise, which plots underperform. Compounds over time into a real quality and cost advantage.

Soil moisture & temperature
How wet and warm the ground is. Continuous sensor. Most critical during shoot emergence (May–July).
Rainfall
Continuous rain gauge. Calibrates irrigation across the 5-month dry season.
Air temperature & humidity
Microclimate under the canopy. Directly affects growth rate.
Soil chemistry (pH + nutrients)
Annual lab samples. Measure soil acidity and levels of nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium (NPK), the three main nutrients plants need.

Field sensors → on-site relay → cloud dashboard, accessible from anywhere.

Deployed in two phases alongside plantation growth: pilot in 2026 with the Bridge round, full network in 2031 with Round 2. Capex and operating costs pending vendor validation.
