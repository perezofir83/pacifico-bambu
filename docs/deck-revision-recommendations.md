# Pacífico Bambu — Original-Deck Revision Recommendations

**Audience:** Internal (Ofir / founders)
**Purpose:** After adding 3 new slides (12 Diversified Revenue, 13 Stress Test, 14 US Market Deep Dive), this document reviews the **original** deck slide-by-slide and recommends changes — minimal where the existing content stands up to scrutiny, surgical where a small tweak strengthens defensibility.
**Date:** May 2026

---

## Executive Summary

After running 5 independent research agents (Colombian farms, Colombian economics, Colombian case studies, US imports, Mexican domestic + alternative end-uses) and direct verification of cited sources (Market Research Future, Fortune Business Insights, Grand View Research, DataMéxico):

- **The original deck is well-sourced.** Citation footers exist on every quantitative slide with active links to primary references (Fortune Business Insights, Market Research Future, Grand View Research, DataMéxico, USITC HTS, USDA APHIS, ICC-ES, SEMARNAT, SENASICA, CONAFOR, Tosepan, VW Proyecto Bambú, Guadua Selecto, Milenio).
- **No critical factual errors identified** in the original 19 slides.
- **The main gap is demand-side validation**, not any individual claim. The original deck modeled supply (110 ha → 130K poles/yr at 400 MXN) but did not show how 130K poles get absorbed across channels at what blended price. The 3 new slides (12, 13, 14) close this gap.
- **Recommended changes to the original deck are LIGHT** — mostly cross-references to the new slides + 4 surgical clarifications. No rewrites needed.

### Slide 3 — Market Opportunity ($77B / $10.2B / $3.6B / ~$1B)
**Status:** Recommend **clarification footnote**, not value change.
**Issue:** The current line "$10.2B US Bamboo Market · $3.6B addressable · ~$1B niche" is sourced (MRF, FBI, GVR, DataMéxico), within market-research consensus range, and defensible. However:
- Market research firms (MRF, FBI, GVR, Persistence, Maximize) define "US bamboo market" broadly to include flooring, furniture, panels, charcoal, textiles — most of which is Chinese Moso engineered product, not addressable for a Mexican Guadua plantation.
- The "~$1B structural niche" subsegment is **not separately disclosed** by any of the cited sources; it is an interpretation.

**Recommended footnote addition** (short, on the same slide):
> *Market totals from MRF / FBI / Grand View include all bamboo product categories. The structural-pole subsegment is not separately disclosed and is currently small (single-digit $M raw imports per year per US Census HS 1401.10) but in early-stage growth. Pacífico's addressable share is detailed in slide 14 (US Market Deep Dive).*

**Why:** Avoids investor pushback ("show me where $1B comes from") while preserving the headline TAM number.

### Slide 20 (formerly Slide 17) — Regulatory / Export Roadmap (SENASICA, USDA APHIS, ICC-ES AC162)
**Status:** Recommend **adding 2 small items**.
**Existing sources:** SEMARNAT, SENASICA, USDA APHIS, Lacey Act, USITC HTS, ICC-ES AC162, FSC.

**Two small additions worth considering**:

1. **Lacey Act Phase VII (effective Dec 1, 2024)** — extended HTS coverage to bamboo products. Cultivated bamboo (which Pacífico is) remains exempt from Lacey declaration, but compliance scrutiny has increased. This is a positive defensible line, not a negative.

2. **ICC-ES + BASE Bahay collaboration (announced 2024)** — global acceptance criteria for structural bamboo expected by 2026-2027. This is a tailwind for Pacífico's 2037 ICC-ES timeline.

**Suggested wording**:
> *Lacey Act Phase VII (Dec 2024): cultivated bamboo exempt; compliance ready. ICC-ES + BASE Bahay 2024 partnership: global structural-bamboo acceptance criteria targeted 2026-2027 — directly aligned with our 2037 ESR timeline.*

**Why:** Two free regulatory tailwinds + one expanded scrutiny item that you handle. Strengthens the regulatory section without rewriting it.

## Sources to Consider Adding (Free, Authoritative)

The original deck cites commercial market research (FBI, MRF, GVR) heavily but underuses free authoritative sources. These add credibility without paid-report cost:

- **USITC DataWeb** — for any US import claim. https://dataweb.usitc.gov/
- **OEC.world** — for HS-code-level trade flows. https://oec.world/
- **TrendEconomy** — for global bamboo trade. https://trendeconomy.com/data/commodity_h2/140110
- **INBAR Trade Overview** (annual PDF) — for global bamboo trade context. https://www.inbar.int/
- **Camargo et al. peer-reviewed Guadua studies** (Springer, SciELO Colombia) — for yield benchmarks more rigorous than FAO press claims.
- **CONAFOR PRONAFOR PFC program page** — for the cost-share mechanism. Already cited indirectly; could be elevated.
- **MEXICO2 voluntary carbon market** — for the carbon credit overlay. https://www.mexico2.com.mx/
