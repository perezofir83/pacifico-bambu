# Pacífico Bambu — Project Memory

## What is this project?
Pacífico Bambu is an agri-industrial bamboo company located in El Azulillo, Candelaria, Oaxaca, Mexico.
The strategic vision: grow and primary-process premium Guadua bamboo, then export high-margin engineered bamboo slats to the US construction market via the nearshoring supply chain.

**Ofir's role:** Managing Partner & Head of Growth — responsible for fundraising, investor relations, pitch deck, business plan, and growth strategy. Compensation is performance-based equity (up to 15%) + 5% cash success fee on capital raised.

## Strategic Thesis
Pacífico Bambu is NOT competing in the low-margin Mexican domestic market.
**The play:** leverage low-cost Mexican labor + premium Guadua bamboo + USMCA trade advantages to become the dominant, most reliable supplier of **Guadua Slats** (primary processed material) for US engineered bamboo manufacturers.

Target niche: **30–50% share** of the US export-grade Guadua slat market (currently fragmented and undersupplied from Mexico).

## Key Risks & Barriers
1. **Biological gestation** — bamboo requires 5–7 years before full production. Working capital bridge is critical.
2. **Logistics cost** — transporting whole bamboo culms is expensive (hollow = "transporting air"). Primary processing on-site (slats) solves this.
3. **Certifications** — US market requires ICC-ES/ASTM standards. This is a moat once achieved.
4. **Minimum export scale** — experts recommend 100+ ha for export viability. Current 10 ha → scaling to 190 ha is the core execution challenge.
5. **Land title** — Juan's land still in grandfather's name; regularization budgeted at 38,000 MXN.
